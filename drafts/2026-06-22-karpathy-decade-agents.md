Andrej Karpathy thinks it will take a decade to work through the fundamental issues with AI agents. I think he is right, and that is actually good news for engineers.

The challenges are not just technical — they are epistemic. Agents fail in ways that are hard to predict, hard to reproduce, and hard to attribute. A system that works cleanly in testing can make a damaging decision in production because the context looked slightly different or a tool returned something unexpected.

What this means for practitioners: the valuable skills right now are not just 'can you deploy an agent' but 'can you build reliable, observable, recoverable agentic systems.' That means thinking about rollback, audit trails, human escalation paths, and failure mode analysis — the same disciplines you would apply to any distributed system, now applied to systems with nondeterministic components.

A decade-long timeline is not pessimism. It is a signal that deep systems engineering knowledge will continue to matter here, and that the people building carefully will outlast the people building fast.
