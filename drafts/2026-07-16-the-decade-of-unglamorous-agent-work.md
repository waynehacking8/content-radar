Karpathy said something recently that cuts against the current mood: it will take a decade to work through the real issues with agents. Not "agents are useless," not "AGI is near." A decade of unglamorous, grinding work between the demo and the dependable system.

That's not a bearish take, it's an honest timeline. Anyone who's shipped an agent into a real workflow already knows the gap: demos handle the happy path, production has fifty edge cases the model has never seen, permission boundaries that need to be exact, and failure modes that need graceful recovery, not just retries.

I actually find that reassuring. If the hard part is genuinely a decade of integration, reliability, and trust-building rather than a single model breakthrough, that's an enormous amount of room for people who are good at exactly that: closing the gap between "the model can do this" and "the business can rely on this."

That gap-closing work is the job, not a stepping stone to the job.
