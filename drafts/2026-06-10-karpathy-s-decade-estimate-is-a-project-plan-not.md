Andrej Karpathy recently said it'll take a decade to work through the issues with AI agents. I think he's right, and it's worth being precise about why.

The capability gap is closing fast. You can already build agents that browse, write and run code, manage files, and call APIs — and they work surprisingly often. The hard part isn't making agents more capable. It's making them trustworthy enough to deploy where mistakes are expensive.

That requires infrastructure we mostly don't have yet: reliable audit trails, per-action authorization models, graceful rollback, anomaly detection for agentic behavior, and — critically — organizational processes for humans to review agent work faster than it causes damage.

We're not waiting on better language models. We're waiting on the ops and governance layer that lets organizations actually trust autonomous systems with real consequences.

The decade estimate isn't doom. It's an honest scope of work. If you're building in this space, that infrastructure layer is where the durable value gets created.
