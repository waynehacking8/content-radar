Karpathy said something worth sitting with: working through the real issues with AI agents will take a decade. Not because the models aren't impressive—they are—but because the problems aren't mostly technical. They're about trust, reliability, error recovery, and the social infrastructure around delegating decisions to software.

I think he's right. And I think that makes right now the most interesting moment to be working in this space.

When a technology is mature, the job is optimization. When it's in the messy middle—capable enough to deploy, unreliable enough to require constant oversight—the job is systems thinking. What breaks? Under what conditions? Who's accountable when it does? How do you build confidence incrementally rather than betting everything on a single demo?

That's not a model training problem. It's a deployment, product, and architecture problem. Engineers who understand both the capabilities and the failure modes are going to matter a lot over the next ten years. The messy middle is where the interesting work lives.
