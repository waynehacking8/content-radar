Andrej Karpathy said it plainly in a recent interview: it will probably take a decade to work through the problems with agents. I think he's right, and not because the models aren't capable enough.

The hard problems aren't benchmark numbers. They're things like: what happens when an agent is 80% through a multi-step task and encounters something outside its design envelope? Who is accountable when an autonomous system takes a consequential wrong action? How do you write reliable evals for long-horizon tasks where ground truth is ambiguous?

These are sociotechnical problems, not purely technical ones. They require new tooling, new organizational habits, and new frameworks for liability that don't exist yet. Model improvements will keep coming — that part is predictable. The surrounding infrastructure, trust models, and governance lag by years.

If you're deploying agents in production today, you're doing applied research whether you've framed it that way or not.
