Andrej Karpathy said recently that it will take a decade to work through the real problems with AI agents. My first instinct was to push back — but I think he's right, and for reasons that aren't obvious.

It's not that the models are too weak. Today's frontier models can already reason, plan, and use tools at a level that was science fiction three years ago. The hard part is everything around them: how you scope permissions, how you handle errors that compound across 20 tool calls, how you build user trust after a hallucination, how you debug a workflow where the failure happened six steps back.

These are systems design problems, org design problems, trust problems. None of those get solved fast just because the underlying model improves.

A decade sounds pessimistic on paper. But if you've spent time shipping real agentic systems in production, it sounds pretty honest.
