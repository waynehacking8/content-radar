"AI will transform every industry" is probably true. It's also not useful as an engineering constraint.

LLM inevitabilism — the belief that language models will comprehensively disrupt everything, and that skepticism is just failure to see clearly — has a structural problem: it's unfalsifiable. When a use case works, it confirms the thesis. When one fails, we just haven't waited long enough.

The practitioners I respect ask a narrower question: where, specifically, does this tool produce better outcomes than what we already have? Not eventually, not theoretically — in the actual system, with the actual users, measurably.

That framing forces honesty. Some LLM applications are genuinely transformative right now. Others are expensive demos that haven't survived contact with production data or real users. Distinguishing between the two is the actual job — for engineers, for architects, and for anyone advising organizations on where to invest.

Hype isn't your enemy. Uncritical hype is. Keep the bar high.
