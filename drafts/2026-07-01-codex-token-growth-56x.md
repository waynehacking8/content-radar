OpenAI reported that median Codex output tokens grew 56x in Research, 32x in Customer Support, 27x in Engineering, and 13x in Legal — all since November 2025.

People are reading this as "AI is getting more capable." I'd read it differently: teams are finally learning how to use agents.

Token volume is a proxy for task complexity. When outputs grow 56x, it's because someone stopped asking the model to autocomplete a function and started asking it to research a topic, draft a report, or coordinate across tools. The model didn't change — the prompts and workflows did.

This is the angle that doesn't get enough attention in enterprise AI conversations: deploying agents isn't about picking the right model. It's about designing workflows where the agent has enough context, clear enough scope, and the right tools to do something genuinely useful. That takes iteration and someone who understands both the business problem and the system design.

The teams seeing 56x growth found that pattern. The ones still at 1x haven't.
