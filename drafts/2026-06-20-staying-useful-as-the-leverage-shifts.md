Two loud camps dominate the LLM career conversation: "AI will automate everything and your job is gone" and "LLMs are just autocomplete and can't reason." Both are positions that let people stop thinking.

The more honest framing: LLMs are genuinely reshaping where leverage lives in software development. Work that took a junior engineer a day can take an hour with a good agent setup. That's real and it compounds.

But understanding systems, scoping problems correctly, and knowing when the output is subtly wrong — that work is harder to automate, and its value is going up, not down.

The engineers I see thriving aren't ignoring AI tools or fully offloading their thinking to them. They're using AI to amplify the parts of the job that were already table stakes, while doubling down on the judgment-heavy work that still requires a person who understands the whole system.

The skill is staying useful as the leverage shifts.
