Andrej Karpathy said something recently that I keep coming back to: it'll take about a decade to work through the actual issues with agents, not because the models aren't smart enough, but because the surrounding infrastructure, trust, and tooling don't exist yet. That tracks with what I see in practice. The demo is never the hard part. The hard part is what happens after the demo: who reviews the agent's output, what happens when it's confidently wrong, how you roll back an action it already took, how you explain its decision to a compliance team. None of that is a model problem. It's a systems and process problem, and it's exactly the kind of problem that doesn't show up until you try to put something in front of a real user with real stakes. If you're evaluating an AI vendor or building agent workflows internally, budget for this. The pilot working is the easy 20%. Plan the rest like an infrastructure project, because that's what it is.
