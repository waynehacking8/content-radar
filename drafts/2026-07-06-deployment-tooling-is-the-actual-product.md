Google just shipped agents-cli, a command-line tool that turns whatever coding assistant you already use into something that can build, evaluate, and deploy agents on their enterprise agent platform. What I like about it isn't the novelty — it's what it reveals about where the real work has moved. Getting a demo agent working is maybe 20% of the job now; frameworks and models have gotten good enough that a working prototype is almost table stakes. The other 80% is evaluation, deployment, access control, and making the thing survive contact with a real enterprise environment. That's not glamorous work, but it's exactly the skill set that separates people who ship agents from people who demo them. If you're building toward a solutions architect or forward-deployed role, spend less time chasing the newest framework and more time getting fluent with whatever your target platform uses to actually get agents into production and keep them there.
