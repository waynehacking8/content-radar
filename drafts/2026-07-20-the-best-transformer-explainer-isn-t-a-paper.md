If you've ever tried to explain what an LLM is actually doing to a non-ML stakeholder, bbycroft.net/llm is the best tool I've found for it. It's an interactive, step-by-step visualization of a GPT-style model: tokens flowing through embeddings, attention, and MLP layers, rendered so you can watch the computation happen instead of just reading about it.

The value isn't for engineers who already know the architecture, it's for the room full of people who don't: product managers, execs, customers evaluating whether to trust a model with their workflow. Words like "attention" and "context window" stop being jargon the moment someone can see tokens actually attending to each other.

In solutions and forward-deployed roles, half the job is building trust with people who aren't going to read a transformer paper. Having one good visual explanation in your back pocket does more for a conversation than three slides of architecture bullet points.

Worth bookmarking even if you already understand the internals. It's a better explainer than most of us could build from scratch.
