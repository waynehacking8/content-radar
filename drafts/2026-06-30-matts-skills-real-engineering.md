The best AI-assisted engineers I know have one thing in common: they've built opinionated tooling on top of their tools.

Matt Pocock just published the skills directory he actually uses day-to-day with Claude — not tutorial prompts, not "write me a function" templates, but structured agent skills that encode real engineering judgment. Things like how to scope a refactor, how to handle spec drift, when to push back on a requirement.

The interesting thing isn't the specific skills. It's the pattern: treating your AI configuration as a first-class engineering artifact that you maintain, version, and improve. That's a different posture than reaching for a prompt when you need something.

If you're serious about AI-assisted development, your setup should reflect your experience level. A senior engineer's system should behave differently than a junior engineer's. Pocock's repo is a good example of what that looks like in practice — skills that encode judgment, not just instructions.
