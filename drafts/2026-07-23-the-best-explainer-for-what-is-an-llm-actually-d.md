One of the hardest parts of moving into a customer-facing AI role is explaining transformers to someone who doesn't want the math, just the intuition. The LLM Visualization tool at bbycroft.net is the best resource I've found for this — it walks through a GPT-style model step by step, showing tokens turning into embeddings, attention heads lighting up, and logits turning into the next predicted word, all rendered as an interactive 3D diagram you can rotate and step through.

What makes it genuinely useful in a work setting, not just a curiosity, is that it gives you a shared visual to point at when a stakeholder asks "but how does it actually decide what to say next?" Instead of reaching for analogies that oversimplify or equations that lose the room, you can show the actual mechanism at a pace people can follow.

If you explain AI systems to non-ML audiences for a living, bookmark it.
