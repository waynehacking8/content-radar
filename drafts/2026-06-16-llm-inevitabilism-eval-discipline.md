There's a rhetorical pattern in AI discourse worth naming: inevitabilism. The argument goes that LLMs will reach some future capability level, therefore current criticisms are shortsighted, therefore the right response to any limitation is patience.

The problem is that inevitabilism is unfalsifiable. It reframes every present failure as temporary and every skeptic as someone who just doesn't see the arc of history. That's not an argument — it's a debate technique that makes the conversation impossible.

For practitioners, this matters because it bleeds into how we evaluate tools. "It will be smarter in two years" is not an answer to "does it reliably handle this workflow today?" Deployment decisions require present-tense evaluation against real requirements, not projections. You can hold both things at once: genuine optimism about where this is heading and rigorous honesty about what it does right now. The second part is actually the harder discipline.
