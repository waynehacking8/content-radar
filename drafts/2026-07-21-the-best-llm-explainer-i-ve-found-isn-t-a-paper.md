If you want to actually see what happens inside a transformer instead of just nodding along to the words 'attention mechanism,' bbycroft's LLM Visualization is the best resource I've found. It walks a GPT-style model step by step, showing tokens turning into vectors, attention heads lighting up, and the forward pass moving through the network, all rendered so you can watch it happen instead of imagining it from a diagram. I keep coming back to it when I need to explain to a non-ML engineer why a model behaves the way it does, or when I want to re-anchor my own intuition after spending too much time at the API layer. There's a real gap between 'I use LLMs daily' and 'I could sketch what's happening inside one,' and tools like this are how you close it without reading a textbook. Worth quietly showing to your team, especially anyone client-facing who fields the 'so how does this actually work' question.
