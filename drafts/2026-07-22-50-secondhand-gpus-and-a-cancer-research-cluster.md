My favorite AI-adjacent story this month isn't about a new model, it's about a programmer who built a cancer research compute cluster out of 50 secondhand Nvidia 1080Ti GPUs. No cutting-edge hardware, no cloud contract, just older consumer cards wired together and pointed at a real scientific problem. The lesson generalizes further than GPUs: most compute problems people bring to me aren't actually solved by newer, bigger hardware. They're solved by better orchestration of what's already sitting around underused. A pile of older machines, scheduled well, beats a shiny cluster that's half idle. That's an unglamorous truth, but it's the honest one, and it's usually the cheaper one too. Before recommending a hardware upgrade or a bigger cloud bill, it's worth asking what's already available and whether the bottleneck is really capacity, or just coordination.
