Andrej Karpathy said it'll take a decade to work through the real problems with agents. Some people read that as bearish. I read it as the most useful framing anyone senior in AI has offered this year.

We're not waiting on a capability breakthrough. Models can already plan, use tools, and recover from errors — sometimes impressively well. What we don't have yet is the infrastructure layer that makes agents reliable in production: robust memory, verifiable tool use, stable principal hierarchies, audit trails, and failure modes that degrade gracefully instead of silently.

That's not an AI research problem. It's a software engineering problem. And software engineering problems take time not because they're unsolvable, but because they require real-world feedback loops, edge case discovery, and iteration at scale.

A decade means this is a real engineering career, not a gold rush. If you're building the plumbing now, that work compounds.
