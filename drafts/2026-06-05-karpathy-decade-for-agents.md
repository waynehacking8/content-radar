Andrej Karpathy said it'll take a decade to work through the real issues with AI agents. A lot of people pushed back. I think he's right, and it's actually useful framing if you're building in this space.

The problem isn't capability — agents can already do impressive things. The problem is reliability. A coding agent that succeeds 90% of the time is great in a demo. In production, that's one failure every ten tasks, compounding across a workflow with dozens of steps.

Reliability at production scale requires something we largely don't have yet: agents that recognize when they're about to do something wrong and stop. That's a harder problem than getting them to succeed when things go well.

A decade sounds pessimistic. I read it as: the research direction is clear, the infrastructure is being built, but early wins are not solved problems. If you're deploying agents today, design explicitly for the 10% failure case — not just the 90% success case.
