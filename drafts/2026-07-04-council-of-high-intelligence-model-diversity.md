The quiet problem with single-model AI systems: the model's blind spots are consistent. Ask the same hard question ten times and you'll get ten answers that fail in the same direction.

Council of High Intelligence runs 18 AI personas — Aristotle, Feynman, Kahneman, Torvalds and others — through structured multi-round deliberation across multiple LLM providers. The output is a synthesized answer with visible disagreement surfaced, not a single confident reply.

The interesting part isn't the personas — it's the model diversity. Different foundation models have genuinely different training distributions and failure modes. Running a high-stakes question through Claude, GPT, and Gemini in parallel and noting where they disagree is a real quality signal, not theater.

For decisions where being confidently wrong is expensive, structured deliberation across diverse models is an underused pattern. It costs more tokens and takes longer. The calibration improvement is real.
