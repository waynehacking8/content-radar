Andrej Karpathy said it'll take a decade to work through the issues with agents. That's not pessimism — it's useful calibration.

The hype cycle says agents are here. The engineering reality is that we're still figuring out trust, observability, and failure modes. Most agent systems I work with are brittle at the edges: they work beautifully on the demo path and break in predictable-but-unaddressed ways on anything slightly off-script.

What Karpathy is pointing at isn't that agents won't be valuable — they already are. It's that the discipline of building agents reliably, with the same rigor we apply to distributed systems or security-sensitive code, is still being invented.

A decade of infrastructure, tooling, and accumulated best practices is plausible. That's not a reason to wait. It's a reason to build carefully, instrument everything, and be honest with customers about what this generation can and can't do reliably.

Ship with guardrails. Iterate in the open.
