Andrej Karpathy said it'll take a decade to fully work through the problems with AI agents. I think he's right, and I also think it's the most useful framing I've heard on this topic.

People tend to read that as pessimism. I read it as a roadmap. A decade is roughly how long it took the industry to get cloud infrastructure right — we went from "VMs are scary" to "serverless is boring" over about ten years of painful, expensive lessons. The same arc is coming for agents: early hype, high-profile failures, gradual tooling, then quiet reliability.

The engineers who do well in that arc aren't the ones betting on the hype cycle or against it. They're the ones building real things now, hitting the real failure modes early, and learning what "production-ready" actually means for this technology before the market demands it. The decade of work ahead is an opportunity, not a delay.
