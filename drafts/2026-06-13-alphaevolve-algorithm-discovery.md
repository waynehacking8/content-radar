For 50 years, the best-known algorithm for multiplying 4x4 matrices required 49 scalar multiplications. AlphaEvolve, a Gemini-powered coding agent from DeepMind, found one that needs 48.

That might sound incremental. But this is a problem hundreds of mathematicians and computer scientists have worked on since Strassen's 1969 breakthrough. The agent didn't brute-force it — it evolved candidate programs, evaluated them against a correctness oracle, and iterated on the ones that survived. DeepMind reports similar gains in data center scheduling and chip design: domains where the solution space is too large for human intuition to explore efficiently.

I'm not ready to say AI replaces algorithm designers. But I do think any engineer working on performance-critical systems should update their prior on where better solutions might come from. The search space for optimization problems just got meaningfully larger than what human reasoning alone can cover.
